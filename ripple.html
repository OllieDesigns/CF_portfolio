Ripple

Ripple is an app designed to help users build habits and take control of their mental, emotional, and physical health.

Research

Reasearch was conducted throughout the process of designing Ripple but started with a competitive analysis of four different popular mental health apps to assess any gaps in the market and the apps' weaknesses. Following initial research, goals and associated customer and stakeholder needs were addressed in a business requirements document. User interviews were also used to form a basis for user personas and their associated journeys.

Ideation

Based on the information gathered in the research phase, an initial sitemap was created in based on the initial low fidelity wireframe sketches. The sitemap was then improved via cards sorts in OptimalSort with five participants. Following the created of the updated sitemap, low fidelity wireframes reflecting the new sitemap were drafted and highlighted to show user paths.

Wireframing

Low fidelity wireframes were initially created using paper and pencil as a basis for iterations. With the progression of the wireframes and additional research, the main functions of Ripple changed to remove the analytics functions in favor of a more clearly defined and dedicated habit tracking function. During user interviews, interviewees mentioned being unmotivated by analytical pages that are too upfront and wanting more focus on an expanded habit section in order to stay focused and motivated.

Both mid and high fidelity wireframes were created in Figma, while all low fidelity iterations were drafted on paper and later on Procreate.

Conclusion

Additional Hypotheses

  • Offer option to select an appointment to be associared with a document when uploading said document. The objective is to making finding a specific document easer for users; redusing user frustration and cognitive load
  • Offer a tagging system for appointments and documentas that can be searched or selected from a list for ease of access. The objective is to make finding a specific item easily achievable by allowing users to search items grouped by tags.
  • Revise document search UI. The objective is to create a UI that uses hierarcyh and floating action buttons effectively to clearly indicate what actions a user should take in order to search their documents.

User Orientated Improvements

  • Add a comprehensive progrssive onborading throughout the application to clearly explain functions. Additionally add option to reset tutorial or dismiss completely for certain areas and functions of the application.
  • Add mental health resources section for users needing specific access to verified treatment options and resources.

Further Testing

  • Further testing will evolve by focusing less on adding new functions and more on deep refinement of existing functions via task testing. Among other items, details such as accessibility, contrast, and verbiage would be further improved upon.
  • Ripple would benefit from additional quantitative data which would be accumulated via further user interviews in order to understand more about the target audience, their needs, behaviors, and goals and how those change over time.
